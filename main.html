Steven Jobs

The man in the machine

Here's a time line of Steven Job's life:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California
  • 1972 - He attended Reed College, at once dropping out that same year.
  • 1974 - traveled through India and seeking enlightenment and studying Zen Buddhism.
  • 1976 - Jobs and Wozniak co-founded Apple, and sold Wozniak's Apple I personal computer.
  • 1979 - Jobs saw the commercial potential of the Xerox Alto, which was mouse-driven and had a graphical user interface (GUI).
  • 1983 - This led to the development of the unsuccessful Apple Lisa.
  • 1984 - the first mass-produced computer with a GUI.
  • 1985 - The Macintosh introduced the desktop publishing industry with the addition of the Apple LaserWriter, the first laser printer to feature vector graphics, and Jobs was forced out of Apple.
  • 1995 - The new company was Pixar, which produced the first 3D computer animated feature film Toy Story.
  • 1997 - Steve Jobs returned to apple after they bought a controlling share of NeXT.
  • 2001 - the original Mac OS was replaced with the completely new Mac OS X (now known as macOS), based on NeXT's NeXTSTEP platform, giving the OS a modern Unix-based foundation for the first time.
  • 2003 - Jobs was diagnosed with a pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor
  • 2005 - Job began work on the first iPhone in apple.
  • 2007 Jobs unveil the iPhone at MacWorld Conference & Expo on.
  • 2011 - dies at the age of 56.
